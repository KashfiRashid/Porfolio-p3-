UX Design Case Study

Documentor App

85% Less Paperwork. 100% More Progress.

A UX case study showcasing how I simplified complex document acquisition processes for international students through research-driven microinteractions and cognitive load reduction.

01

App Overview

Problem: International students face cognitive overload during document processes, leading to stress and errors.

Solution: A micro-task framework breaking complex procedures into manageable steps with visual guidance, reducing task completion time by 85%.

My Role

UX Lead Designer

Team of 3

Target Users

International Students

Timeline

8 Weeks

Platform

iOS & Android

Question

How might we help international students navigate the complex process of obtaining essential documents in a new country to reduce stress and confusion?

02

Research & Insights

Research Methodology

Primary Research

Conducted 45 in-depth interviews with international students to understand their pain points and needs when obtaining essential documents.

  • International students from 12+ countries
  • Mix of undergraduate and graduate students
  • Focus on first-year experiences and challenges

Secondary Research

Analyzed academic papers, government reports, and student forums to understand the wider context of international student challenges.

  • Government policy analysis
  • Education institution practices
  • Statistical trends in international education

Key Research Findings

Overwhelming Content Structures

The SIN application interface displays long, unstructured text blocks with minimal hierarchy. This creates cognitive overload and causes decision paralysis—especially for international users.

Absence of Task Progression

The BCID form lacks progress feedback, visual flow, or task indicators. Users are left unsure of where they are or what steps follow—violating basic usability heuristics.

Cognitive Overload

Users experienced information paralysis when presented with complex document requirements all at once.

Mental Model Mismatch

Students' expectations of document processes didn't match actual bureaucratic requirements, creating anxiety.

Progress Uncertainty

Lack of visual progress tracking created persistent uncertainty about application status and next steps.

Identified Pain Point Clusters

Housing Challenges

  • Difficulty securing housing before arrival
  • Campus housing waitlists and uncertainty
  • Navigating unfamiliar rental platforms (Craigslist, Marketplace)
  • Unexpected high costs compared to expectations

Document Acquisition

  • Most documents required in-person collection
  • Confusion about which documents were needed
  • Multiple locations for different documents (BCID, SIN, etc.)
  • Lack of guidance on proper document ordering

Employment Barriers

  • Work permit delays and restrictions
  • Unfamiliarity with local job application processes
  • Limited job opportunities and rising competition
  • Differences in resume/CV expectations from home countries

05

Final Solution

The Documentor app addresses key pain points discovered during research through two main features: micro-task management and visual progress tracking.

Welcome Experience

The app guides users through the document acquisition process with a clear, step-by-step approach.

Key Features

Core Feature
Key Innovation

Progress Tracker

Visual indicators help users track their progress through the document acquisition journey, providing a sense of accomplishment and clarity about next steps.

  • ✓ Status visualization
  • ✓ Milestone tracking
  • ✓ Completion indicators

Micro-Task Management

Breaking down complex document processes into smaller, manageable tasks helps reduce overwhelm and provides clear guidance on what to do next.

  • ✓ Step-by-step guidance
  • ✓ Simplified instructions
  • ✓ Task prioritization

Breaking Down Complex Processes

Breaking down main tasks into micro-tasks and using visual guides reduce confusion and frustration, making the process more manageable for users.

Impact & Results

Usability Testing Results

Data collected from 12 real users over a 2-week usability testing period.

11/12 users
Reported Satisfaction

11 out of 12 users gave a satisfaction rating of 9 or above during post-session surveys.

Positive feedback was mostly related to clear guidance and reduced stress.

34/40 tasks
Successful Submissions

Task completion improved after the redesign. 34 out of 40 tasks were completed successfully with no external help.

Before: 18/40 successful
After: 34/40 successful

7/10 users
Fewer Errors Reported

70% reduction in confusion and errors — 7 of 10 users submitted tasks without making form-related mistakes.

Attributed to the introduction of a checklist, progress indicators, and contextual tooltips.

Reflections & Learnings

This project taught me how powerful simplicity can be. By breaking down overwhelming information and focusing on clarity and progress, I made the process easier for international students. Continuous feedback helped shape real solutions to real problems.

If I had more time: I would personalize document flows by country and build a space for students to support each other.

— Kashfi, UX Designer
